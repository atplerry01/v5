## REPLAY-DETERMINISM AUDIT — CRITICAL FINDING

The replay-determinism audit ([replay-determinism.audit.md:53-72](claude/audits/replay-determinism.audit.md#L53-L72)) **explicitly documents** that the sentinel values `PolicyHash="replay"`, `ExecutionHash="replay"`, and `Timestamp=DateTimeOffset.MinValue` in `EventReplayService.cs` are **by design**, not a bug:

> "This is **by design** — the sentinel signals to downstream consumers that the envelope is a rebuild artifact, not a fresh execution... If a future requirement needs full envelope-field equality on rebuild, `EventReplayService` would need to load the original envelopes from the store rather than reconstructing them. **That is a design change, not a fix.**"

**Implication:** the original prompt's Step 3 was not just structurally wrong (the issue I caught at pre-flight) — it would also have **violated documented design intent**. The deferral to "Prompt B" was incorrect. **Step 3 should be DROPPED entirely** unless a separate, justified design change request comes through with a new requirement.

This is a memory-worthy finding: future passes that propose "fixing" `EventReplayService` sentinels should be challenged against this audit.

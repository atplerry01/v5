# Phase 6 — Intelligence, Automation, and Hardening

## Canonical role
- Phase 6 begins only after the deterministic system is proven operationally.
- It is not a foundation-building phase.
- It is not a domain-model phase.
- It is not where unresolved core architecture gets invented.
- It is the phase where Whycespace introduces controlled intelligence, automation, simulation-driven governance, and deeper hardening on top of an already-proven system.

## Core mission
- Introduce T3I safely.
- Add bounded automation without violating deterministic execution.
- Add simulation-driven decision support and policy testing.
- Strengthen system hardening for hostile environments, scale, abuse, and recovery.
- Preserve constitutional supremacy: intelligence must advise, classify, simulate, recommend, or orchestrate within policy bounds, never replace policy truth.

## Entry conditions
- Phase 1 complete and proven.
- Phase 1.5 hardening/certification complete and evidenced.
- Phase 2 complete and certified.
- Phase 3 complete and all non-T3I domains implemented end to end.
- Phase 4 workflow completion done.
- Phase 5 operational activation proven with multi-cluster execution and HEOS implementation.
- Deterministic runtime, enforcement, workflow integrity, observability, and recovery all proven before any intelligence layer is activated.

## What Phase 6 is really about
- Moving from deterministic system execution to policy-bounded intelligence-assisted execution.
- Adding machine assistance only where system truth, enforcement, and auditability already exist.
- Building automation that can never outrun identity, policy, workflow, economic controls, or evidence logging.
- Turning the platform from operationally complete into operationally adaptive, while remaining constitution-first.

## Primary workstreams

### 1. T3I architecture activation
- Introduce the T3I layer formally into the runtime ecosystem.
- Define exactly which intelligence engines exist.
- Separate deterministic engines from advisory/intelligence engines.
- Ensure T3I never becomes a hidden decision-maker outside WHYCEPOLICY.
- Define clear invocation boundaries between T0U, T1M, T2E, and T3I.

### 2. Intelligence role classification
- Advisory intelligence.
- Classification intelligence.
- Detection intelligence.
- Recommendation intelligence.
- Forecast intelligence.
- Simulation intelligence.
- Assisted orchestration intelligence.
- Exception-triage intelligence.
- Each role must be explicitly bounded and policy-governed.

### 3. Policy-bounded automation
- Automate only actions allowed by WHYCEPOLICY.
- Require actor identity, execution classification, and evidence logging for every automated action.
- Prevent silent autonomous execution.
- Support human-in-the-loop, quorum-gated, and policy-gated automation modes.
- Define escalation rules for automation refusal, pause, rollback, and review.

### 4. Simulation workflows
- Policy simulation before policy deployment.
- Workflow simulation before high-risk orchestration changes.
- Economic simulation before capital-routing or enforcement changes.
- Institutional simulation before structural expansion.
- Adversarial simulation for abuse, fraud, and failure-path analysis.
- Simulation results must be evidence-linked and non-authoritative until approved.

### 5. Decision-support systems
- Build systems that help operators, stewards, and governance actors understand likely consequences.
- Support impact forecasting.
- Show trade-offs and policy conflicts.
- Provide confidence-scored recommendations.
- Surface reasoning artifacts and evidence references.
- Never allow decision-support outputs to become constitutional truth automatically.

### 6. Anomaly and abuse detection
- Bot-pattern detection.
- Fraud-pattern detection.
- Identity-behavior anomaly detection.
- Economic abuse detection.
- Workflow abuse detection.
- Structural misuse detection.
- Policy evasion pattern detection.
- Cross-cluster anomaly correlation.
- All detections must flow into policy, enforcement, audit, and review pipelines.

### 7. Autonomous systems safeguards
- Sandboxed internal AI agents only.
- No unrestricted autonomy.
- No direct authority over funds, identity, or constitutional rules.
- Require bounded tool access.
- Require policy-evaluated permissions.
- Require full traceability of prompts, context, outputs, and actions.
- Require kill-switches, throttles, and supervised escalation.

### 8. Guidance and recommendation systems
- Guidance implementation that was deferred from Phase 2 can live here.
- Operational guidance.
- Governance guidance.
- Economic guidance.
- Workflow guidance.
- Risk guidance.
- Partner/steward/operator support.
- Guidance must remain advisory unless explicitly promoted into policy-bound execution flows.

### 9. Hardening against hostile automation
- Extend the bot-hostile-by-default doctrine.
- Detect scripted abuse and coordinated automation.
- Rate-limit by trust, context, and policy class.
- Add adaptive challenge systems.
- Add suspicious workflow throttling.
- Add hostile actor graphing and containment.
- Add progressive restriction and quarantine modes.

### 10. Runtime intelligence observability
- Track intelligence inputs, outputs, confidence, latency, action impact, and drift.
- Observe false positives and false negatives.
- Maintain audit trails for automated recommendations and actions.
- Monitor model behavior over time.
- Detect degraded intelligence quality.
- Support rollback or disablement of misbehaving intelligence components.

### 11. Intelligence governance
- Define who can deploy, tune, approve, suspend, or retire intelligence systems.
- Add constitutional and policy constraints for intelligence use.
- Add quorum or guardian control for high-impact intelligence classes.
- Require simulation and approval before production activation for sensitive intelligence paths.

### 12. Knowledge and context management
- Controlled retrieval and context assembly for internal intelligence functions.
- Domain-safe knowledge access.
- Context provenance tracking.
- Evidence-linked recommendation generation.
- Prevent context poisoning and unauthorized retrieval.

### 13. Recovery and fail-safe behavior
- Intelligence layer failure must not break deterministic core execution.
- System must degrade safely when intelligence systems are unavailable.
- T3I outputs must be optional unless explicitly required by policy.
- Hard fallback to deterministic pathways must always exist.

### 14. Security and red-team hardening
- Prompt-injection resistance for internal agents.
- Context isolation.
- Tool permission isolation.
- Output validation.
- Adversarial testing.
- Abuse simulation.
- Insider misuse detection.
- Automated action rollback pathways where possible.

### 15. Certification and production-readiness proof
- Intelligence safety certification.
- Automation-boundary certification.
- Simulation fidelity validation.
- Abuse-detection quality validation.
- Fail-safe degradation proof.
- Evidence and audit completeness proof.
- Human override proof.
- Policy supremacy proof.

## What must be deferred even inside Phase 6 unless tightly controlled
- Fully autonomous constitutional amendment behavior.
- Unbounded agentic execution across domains.
- Self-modifying policy engines without approval.
- Self-authorizing economic movement.
- Hidden scoring systems affecting users without governance visibility.
- Opaque black-box decisions with no audit or evidence path.

## Recommended Phase 6 topic structure
- Phase 6.1 — T3I architecture and engine boundaries
- Phase 6.2 — simulation infrastructure and scenario engines
- Phase 6.3 — guidance and recommendation systems
- Phase 6.4 — anomaly, fraud, and hostile-automation detection
- Phase 6.5 — supervised automation and agent framework
- Phase 6.6 — intelligence observability, drift, and fail-safe controls
- Phase 6.7 — intelligence governance, guardian controls, and certification

## Success criteria
- T3I is active but constitutionally subordinate.
- Automation cannot bypass identity, policy, workflow, or evidence systems.
- Every automated or intelligence-assisted action is attributable and reviewable.
- Simulation becomes a first-class pre-deployment and pre-decision capability.
- Guidance improves operator effectiveness without becoming hidden authority.
- Hostile automation is detected, constrained, and auditable.
- The system becomes more adaptive without becoming less deterministic at its core.

## Canonical caution
- Phase 6 must not be used to compensate for unfinished core design.
- Intelligence must be layered on top of proven truth, not used to invent missing truth.
- If deterministic execution is not already stable, Phase 6 becomes dangerous instead of valuable.
